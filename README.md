<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" style="margin:0.5em; background:#cef2e0; font-family:inherit; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:0.1em 0.6em;"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;"><div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 120px;">
<a href="/wiki/File:Onychopterella_kokomoensis_holotype.png" class="image" title="O.&#160;kokomoensis"><img alt="O.&#160;kokomoensis" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Onychopterella_kokomoensis_holotype.png/120px-Onychopterella_kokomoensis_holotype.png" decoding="async" width="120" height="194" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Onychopterella_kokomoensis_holotype.png/180px-Onychopterella_kokomoensis_holotype.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Onychopterella_kokomoensis_holotype.png/240px-Onychopterella_kokomoensis_holotype.png 2x" data-file-width="925" data-file-height="1492" /></a><div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;"><i>O.&#160;kokomoensis</i></div></div>
</div>
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/Onychopterella" title="Onychopterella">Onychopterella</a></b></i> was a <a href="/wiki/Predation" title="Predation">predatory</a> aquatic <a href="/wiki/Arthropod" title="Arthropod">arthropod</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Order_(biology)" title="Order (biology)">order</a> of <a href="/wiki/Eurypterid" title="Eurypterid">eurypterids</a>, often called sea scorpions. <a href="/wiki/Fossil" title="Fossil">Fossils</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> <i>O.&#160;kokomoensis</i> <i>(pictured)</i> and <i>O.&#160;pumilus</i> have been found in the United States, and fossils of <i>O.&#160;augusti</i> in South Africa. <i>Onychopterella</i> (from Greek for 'claw wing') lived from the Late <a href="/wiki/Ordovician" title="Ordovician">Ordovician</a> to the Late <a href="/wiki/Silurian" title="Silurian">Silurian</a>, from 444 to 422&#160;million years ago. The head was almost rectangular, with bean-shaped <a href="/wiki/Compound_eye" title="Compound eye">compound eyes</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Arthropod_leg" title="Arthropod leg">limbs</a> were generally long and narrow with a <a href="/wiki/Spine_(zoology)" title="Spine (zoology)">spine</a> on the tip, and the body was <a href="/wiki/Biological_ornament" title="Biological ornament">ornamented</a> with small, pointed <a href="/wiki/Scale_(anatomy)" title="Scale (anatomy)">scales</a>. Lengths ranged from 16&#160;cm (6.3&#160;in) for <i>O.&#160;kokomoensis</i> to 4&#160;cm (1.6&#160;in) for <i>O.&#160;pumilus</i>. <i>Onychopterella</i> was able to swim, and probably able to walk on the seabed with its spines and dig with its head. The best-preserved specimens of <i>O.&#160;augusti</i> show similarities to modern <a href="/wiki/Scorpion" title="Scorpion">scorpions</a> in their <a href="/wiki/Gastrointestinal_tract" title="Gastrointestinal tract">alimentary canal</a>, limb musculature and <a href="/wiki/Respiratory_system" title="Respiratory system">respiratory system</a>. (<b><a href="/wiki/Onychopterella" title="Onychopterella">Full&#160;article...</a></b>)
</p>

<td style="padding:0 6px 0 0">
<p>The underground station <a href="/wiki/Rochusplatz_(KVB)" title="Rochusplatz (KVB)">Rochusplatz</a> on the <b><a href="/wiki/Cologne_Stadtbahn" title="Cologne Stadtbahn">Cologne Stadtbahn</a></b>, a <a href="/wiki/Light_rail" title="Light rail">light rail</a> system in the German city of <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>. The station entrance is at the junction of Venloer Straße with Äußere Kanalstraße in the district of <a href="/wiki/Ehrenfeld,_Cologne" title="Ehrenfeld, Cologne">Ehrenfeld</a>. It was opened in 1992 and consists of a mezzanine and one <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> with two rail tracks. The station was previously known as Äußere Kanalstraße, but was renamed to its present title on 15&#160;December&#160;2019. The system uses pairs of K5000/K5200 units built by <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_Transportation" title="Bombardier Transportation">Bombardier Transportation</a>, which are almost identical to the <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_M5000" title="Bombardier M5000">M5000</a> trams used by <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Metrolink" title="Manchester Metrolink">Metrolink</a> in Manchester, England.
